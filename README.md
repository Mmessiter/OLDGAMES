# '80s Arcade

**▶ Play it in your browser: https://mmessiter.github.io/OLDGAMES/**

Four classics rebuilt from scratch in plain HTML5/JavaScript — no installs, no
internet needed, everything runs locally in your browser.

- **Space Invaders** — marching alien fleet in three colours, destructible
  bunkers, mystery UFO, and that four-note bass heartbeat that speeds up as
  the fleet thins out.
- **Pac-Man** — the full 28×31 maze, four ghosts with their real personalities
  (chaser, ambusher, flanker, shy one), power pellets, fruit, waka-waka.
- **Tetris** — 10×20 well, seven pieces, ghost piece, hard drop, NES-style
  scoring, and Korobeiniki on a square wave.
- **Nebulus** — Pogo the little green fellow climbs a tower rising out of the
  sea, and the famous illusion is intact: he stays centred while the whole
  cylindrical tower rotates around him. Ledges, tunnels through the core,
  lifts, crumbling steps, bouncing baddies, a ticking clock — three towers
  that loop harder.
- **Mr EE** — a loving tribute to the BBC Micro classic (and Mr. Do!). Dig
  tunnels through the garden, gobble the cherry clusters (eight in a row for
  a bonus), push apples onto the creeps chasing you — or pop them with your
  bouncing power-ball. Mind the falling apples.
- **Chess** — a real 3D scene: turned Staunton pieces with polished highlights
  and directional shadows from a lamp fixed in the room, a wood-grain board on
  a round pedestal table sized just right for the board, your captured pieces
  standing beside it as trophies, and a glass of red wine — and your opponent
  (Super-Viktor in his cape, or Elasti-Vera in her mask, press **C** to swap)
  sitting across from you. They stretch out a cartoon arm to move each piece
  by hand, occasionally scratch their head over a hard position (worried
  frown, then a beaming "aha!" smile, then a thoughtful pause before the
  move), and take the odd sip of wine while *you* think. A proper little engine drives
  their play (castling, en passant, promotion — all the rules). Choose White
  or Black; sides alternate each game. Three difficulty levels, undo, pause.

## How to play

**Double-click `Play 80s Arcade.command`** (or just open `index.html` in any
browser — Safari, Chrome, etc.).

> First time only: if macOS says the .command file is from an unidentified
> developer, right-click it → **Open** → **Open**. Opening `index.html`
> directly always works.

Pick a game from the menu with the **arrow keys** and press **ENTER**
(or press **1**–**6**). The menu shows the controls for each game.

### On a phone or tablet
Just open the same link — touch controls appear automatically: a d-pad on
the left (slide your thumb between directions) and action buttons on the
right, plus MENU / PAUSE at the top of the pad. Tap a menu entry to play it.
Chess is played entirely on the board: tap a piece, tap a square, drag to
spin the room, pinch to zoom. For the full arcade feel use Share →
**Add to Home Screen** — you get a proper '80s icon and no browser bars.

### Everywhere
| Key | Action |
|-----|--------|
| ESC | Back to the menu |
| M   | Mute / unmute sound |
| P   | Pause |

### Space Invaders
Left/Right arrows to move, SPACE to fire.

### Pac-Man
Arrow keys to steer.

### Tetris
Left/Right move · Up or X rotate (Z rotates the other way) · Down soft drop ·
SPACE hard drop.

### Nebulus
Left/Right to walk (the tower turns beneath you) · SPACE or Up to jump ·
**walk into** a doorway to pass through the tower, and **step onto** a lift
to ride it — just like the original · **Z**/**X** fires a snowball at the
flashing baddies. Long falls flatten poor Pogo for a moment. Reach the
sparkling door at the top before TIME runs out; the sea below is unforgiving.
Between towers, take the submarine out and **catch fish with bubbles** for
bonus points (Up/Down steer, SPACE fires). The music is an original bouncy
chiptune written for this remake — the 1987 melody belongs to its composer!

### Mr EE
Arrow keys to walk and dig · SPACE throws the power-ball (one at a time — it
bounces down the tunnels until it finds a creep). Walk into an apple to push
it; dig underneath one and it wobbles, then falls — squashing whatever is
below, you included. Clear all the cherries, or squash/pop every creep, to
finish the garden. Creeps that can't reach you eventually turn orange and
dig their own way through!

### Chess
Choose your colour at the start (arrow keys + ENTER, or click) — sides
alternate automatically on each new game. Click a piece, then click its
destination (legal moves are marked); or steer the cursor with the **arrow
keys** and press **ENTER**. **Drag** with the mouse to rotate the 3D board,
**scroll** to zoom, **Q/E** turn, **R/F** tilt, **V** resets the view.
**U** undoes your last move, **P** pauses, **L** cycles the difficulty
(EASY / NORMAL / HARD), **N** starts a new game with the opposite colour.
Pawns promote to queens automatically. On HARD the computer may think for a
few seconds.

High scores (and chess wins) are remembered between sessions (stored in your
browser).

Sound starts after your first key press — browsers require a user gesture
before they allow audio.
